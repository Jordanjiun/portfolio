Linki

I'm quite proud of this project. It's a culmination of everything I've learnt thus far from my job at OneWeb and as a software developer in general. Even though it is just a simple daily web puzzle game in which players try to find the shortest path between two seemingly random Wikipedia pages, this was a proper test of my full-stack capabilities. Built as a React app supported by an AWS backend, I've implemented CI/CD with GitHub Actions, IaC using AWS CDK, and made its entire backend serverless by architecting with AWS. I might have overengineered it, but I definitely had fun creating it. Check out Linki for a new puzzle everyday.

You can get a free copy of Hestian Protocol over at Itch.io.

OneWeb B20

When I was about a year into my job as a spacecraft operator, I had the amazing opportunity to be a part of the final launch of OneWeb's GEN 1 constellation. Batch 20 was a SpaceX mission that took off from Vandenberg Space Force Base to deliver the final 20 satellites of our first cosntellation. It was an experience I won't forget anytime soon as I was Mission Planner and Control-2 for the Launch & Early Orbit Phase (LEOP) which meant that I was in charge of the mission planning system and half of the satellites during and after the launch. There's a ton of other stuff I've also learnt while working for OneWeb in regards to the space industry and backend development which I won't be able to summarise here, but rest assured those shifts would be remembered for a long, long time. You can check out the stream of the launch on YouTube.

Group Design Project

For my final year of university, I was the group leader of the MORPHEUS (Modular Operational Remote Package Handling Electric UAV System) Group Design Project in which our team attempted to design and build a drone that is capable of autonomously detecting, tracking, collecting, and depositing a package on its own based on computer vision. While managing the project, I specialised in the avionics and software components of the system which meant that I had a lot of fun learning about Python's OpenCV and how to use fiducial markers to program and test a control algorithm with a bunch of simulations and eventual in-flight tests. You can check out the code we worked on here, or if you would like to find out more about the overall project, feel free to read our final report.

Individual Project

While completing my degree, my dissertation looked into creating a high-fidelity ground risk model for UAVs. In particular, it explored the possibility of developing a spatiotemporal population model based on publicy available information such as census, workplace, transportation, and healthcare data. This was then used to estimate the risk of fatality that a drone flying overhead might pose to civilians below and how safer flight corridors could be designed by pathfinding an optimised risk/time route. This project is part of the E-Drone Research Programme and you can find my source code here.
